Security Database

Access highly classified terminology and agency intelligence. Filter queries to locate relevant definitions.

Bcrypt

Encryption

A password-hashing function designed to be computationally expensive and intentionally slow, making brute-force and dictionary attacks highly ineffective.

Boundless Informant

Tool

A data analysis tool used by the NSA to summarize and visualize the metadata and communications records they collect globally.

Caesar Cipher

Encryption

Simple substitution cipher where each letter is shifted by a fixed number. Easy to break but historically significant. Example: ROT-3 shifts A to D, B to E, and so on.

CIA — The Obsidian Oversight (Central Intelligence Agency)

Agency

A civilian foreign intelligence service of the federal government of the United States, tasked with gathering, processing, and analyzing national security information. Known in-world as The Obsidian Oversight.

Dictionary Attack

Attack Vector

A method of breaking into a password-protected system by systematically entering every word in a dictionary or a list of common passwords (like rockyou.txt).

Digital Forensics

Concept

The recovery and investigation of material found in digital devices, often used to uncover deleted files, internet history, and hidden metadata.

DNS (Domain Name System)

Concept

The phonebook of the Internet. Translates human-readable domain names (like example.com) into machine-readable IP addresses.

Domestic Surveillance

Surveillance Program

The monitoring of citizens within a country's borders by its own government, often a point of legal and ethical controversy.

Edward Snowden

Surveillance Program

An American former computer intelligence consultant who leaked highly classified information from the NSA in 2013, revealing numerous global surveillance programs.

Encryption Fingerprints

Encryption

Unique patterns or metadata in encrypted traffic that can reveal the type of encryption used, the application generating it, or even the user, without breaking the encryption itself.

EXIF Metadata

Concept

Hidden information embedded in image files, such as the camera model, date/time, and exact GPS coordinates of where the photo was taken.

Firewall

Surveillance Program

Network security system that monitors and filters incoming and outgoing traffic based on predefined rules. Acts as a barrier between trusted and untrusted networks.

FISA (Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act)

Surveillance Program

A United States federal law that establishes procedures for the physical and electronic surveillance and collection of foreign intelligence information.

Foreign Intelligence

Agency

Information relating to the capabilities, intentions, or activities of foreign governments, organizations, or persons.

Hash Function

Encryption

A one-way mathematical algorithm that maps data of arbitrary size to a fixed-size string. You cannot 'decrypt' a hash; you must guess the original input.

HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol)

Surveillance Program

The foundation of data communication for the World Wide Web. It is unencrypted, meaning data sent via HTTP can be intercepted.

HTTPS

Encryption

HTTP with TLS/SSL encryption. Secures data between your browser and a web server. Look for the padlock icon in your browser's address bar.

Inspector General

Concept

An investigative official in a civil or military organization. In the intelligence community, they are responsible for internal audits and investigating whistleblowers' claims.

John the Ripper

Encryption

A fast, open-source password cracking software tool used to perform dictionary attacks and rule-based mutations against password hashes.

Kali Linux

Tool

A Debian-derived Linux distribution designed for digital forensics and penetration testing. It comes pre-installed with hundreds of security and hacking tools.

MAC Address

Concept

A unique hardware identifier assigned to a network interface controller. Used by local networks and routers to identify specific devices.

Man-in-the-Middle Attack

Surveillance Program

An attacker secretly intercepts communication between two parties. Both parties think they're talking directly to each other. Key verification prevents this.

Mass Surveillance

Surveillance Program

The intricate surveillance of an entire or a substantial fraction of a population in order to monitor that group of citizens.

MD5

Encryption

An older, widely used hashing algorithm that is now considered cryptographically broken and vulnerable to rapid brute-force attacks.

Metadata

Concept

Data about data — who you communicated with, when, for how long, from where. Does not include the message content but can reveal intimate patterns of life.

National Security

Agency

The security and defense of a nation state, including its citizens, economy, and institutions, which is regarded as a duty of government.

Network Security

Surveillance Program

Policies and practices adopted to prevent and monitor unauthorized access, misuse, modification, or denial of a computer network and network-accessible resources.

NSA — Signal Reach Network (National Security Agency)

Agency

A national-level intelligence agency of the United States Department of Defense, under the authority of the Director of National Intelligence. Known in-world as the Signal Reach Network.

PGP (Pretty Good Privacy)

Encryption

Asymmetric encryption system using public/private key pairs. Public key encrypts, only the matching private key can decrypt. Used for secure email communication.

Private IP Ranges

Concept

IP addresses reserved for internal network use (like 192.168.x.x) that are not routable on the public internet.

Public Wi-Fi

Surveillance Program

Unsecured wireless networks open to the public. Highly vulnerable to interception and man-in-the-middle attacks.

RDP (Remote Desktop Protocol)

Protocol

A proprietary protocol developed by Microsoft that provides a user with a graphical interface to connect to another computer over a network connection.

Reverse Shell

Attack Vector

A type of shell in which the target machine communicates back to the attacking machine. Often used to bypass firewalls that block incoming connections.

Secure Deletion

Concept

The process of permanently destroying digital data by overwriting the physical disk sectors with random data (e.g., using 'shred'), preventing forensic recovery.

SecureDrop

Concept

An open-source whistleblowing platform that allows anonymous document submission. Used by major news organizations to protect sources.

Session Token

Concept

A unique piece of data generated by a server and stored in a user's browser (often as a cookie) to keep them authenticated after logging in.

SSH (Secure Shell)

Protocol

A cryptographic network protocol for operating network services securely over an unsecured network, often used for remote command-line login.

Telnet

Protocol

An application protocol providing a bidirectional interactive text-oriented communication facility. Unencrypted and largely obsolete for secure networks.

TLS (Transport Layer Security)

Protocol

A cryptographic protocol designed to provide communications security over a computer network, widely used in HTTPS.

TOR (The Onion Router)

Concept

Anonymization network that routes traffic through multiple relay nodes, each encrypting a layer. Makes it very difficult to trace traffic to its source.

Traffic Correlation Attack

Attack Vector

A method used to de-anonymize Tor users by matching the timing and volume of traffic entering the Tor network with traffic exiting it.

USA FREEDOM Act

Concept

A US law enacted in 2015 that restored and modified several provisions of the Patriot Act, imposing some new limits on the bulk collection of telecommunication metadata.

VPN (Virtual Private Network)

Surveillance Program

Creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server, hiding your traffic from local network surveillance. Essential on untrusted networks like public Wi-Fi.

Zero-Day Exploit

Tool

A vulnerability in software unknown to the vendor. Called zero-day because there are zero days of notice before it is exploited. No patch exists yet.

AES-256

Encryption

Advanced Encryption Standard with a 256-bit key size. It is widely considered computationally unbreakable by current technology and is the standard for top-secret government communications.

PRISM

Surveillance Program

A clandestine mass electronic surveillance data mining program. It enabled direct access to the servers of major technology companies to collect communications data without individual warrants.

XKeyscore

Intelligence Tool

A formerly secret computer system used by allied intelligence agencies for searching and analyzing global internet data, providing near real-time tracking of individual users' online activities.

OpSec

Concept

Operations Security. The process of protecting individual pieces of data that could be grouped together to give the bigger picture to an adversary. Critical for whistleblowers avoiding detection.

Bcrypt

Encryption

A password-hashing function designed to be computationally expensive and intentionally slow, making brute-force and dictionary attacks highly ineffective.
